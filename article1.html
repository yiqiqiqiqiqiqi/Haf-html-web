The Fall of the Roman Empire

An in-depth look at the decline of one of history's greatest empires

Economic Challenges

The Roman Empire faced significant economic strain due to overexpansion, heavy taxation, and reliance on slave labor, which weakened its economic foundation by the 3rd century.

Military Decline

Military overreach and the inability to defend vast borders led to invasions by barbarian tribes, notably the Visigoths in 410 CE, marking a critical blow.

Political Instability

Frequent changes in leadership and corruption eroded the empire stability, culminating in the deposition of Romulus Augustulus in 476 CE.
