Iconic Paintings

A curated collection of world-famous paintings

Mona Lisa - Leonardo da Vinci

Italian Renaissance masterpiece, renowned for its mysterious smile and gaze-tracking effect, one of the most famous portraits.

The Last Supper - Leonardo da Vinci

Depicts Jesus and the twelve apostles at their final meal, with intricate composition and intense emotional tension.

Starry Night - Vincent van Gogh

Captures swirling night skies with dynamic energy and emotion, a hallmark of Post-Impressionism.

Sunflowers - Vincent van Gogh

A series showcasing vibrant, passionate life force and personal emotion through vivid yellow blooms.

The Scream - Edvard Munch

Symbolist masterpiece, depicting existential anxiety through a distorted figure and terrified expression.

Liberty Leading the People - Eugène Delacroix

Commemorates the 1830 French July Revolution, with Liberty holding the flag, exuding powerful impact.

Napoleon Crossing the Alps - Jacques-Louis David

Neoclassical work emphasizing Napoleon's heroic and commanding presence.

A Sunday Afternoon on the Island of La Grande Jatte - Georges Seurat

Pointillist classic, using tiny dots to portray modern urban leisure.

The Creation of Adam - Michelangelo

Sistine Chapel ceiling fresco, showing God and Adam's fingers nearly touching, symbolizing the spark of life.

The Birth of Venus - Sandro Botticelli

Early Renaissance work depicting the mythological birth of Venus emerging from the sea.

Guernica - Pablo Picasso

Anti-war protest against the bombing of Guernica, using stark monochrome and chaotic forms.

The Angelus - Jean-François Millet

Portrays peasant farmers praying in the fields, imbued with religious and dignified labor themes.

The Gleaners - Jean-François Millet

Shows poor women gathering leftover grain, symbolizing labor and social class.

The Fifer - Édouard Manet

Impressionist work with flat backgrounds and bold colors, depicting a young military musician.

The Dance Class - Edgar Degas

Captures ballet rehearsal moments, highlighting movement and urban female life.

The Night Watch - Rembrandt

Dutch Golden Age masterpiece, using dramatic light to depict a civic guard patrol.

Girl with a Pearl Earring - Johannes Vermeer

Known as the "Mona Lisa of the North," celebrated for its light and enigmatic expression.

American Gothic - Grant Wood

Satirical yet realistic portrayal of rural American life, a cultural icon.

Café Terrace at Night - Vincent van Gogh

Vividly colored depiction of a nighttime café in Arles, France, with emotional resonance.

The Red Room - Henri Matisse

Fauvist work with decorative composition and rhythmic color patterns.
